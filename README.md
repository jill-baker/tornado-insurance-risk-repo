# tornado-insurance-risk-repo
##Proposed working project title
Shifting Tornado Risk in the United States and Emerging Insurance Exposure

##Brief_background
Tornadoes have traditionally been most prevalent in the Great Plains region called "Tornado Alley" in the United States. However, recent research shows that tornado activity may be shifting eastward toward the Southeast's "Dixie Alley" region. This shift would have implications for the insurance industry with impacts to future risk and loss given the changing patterns.

##Question
To what extent does the historical tornado data support the spatial shift of increased tornado frequency from Tornado Alley to Dixie Alley, and if so, what are the potential implications of that shift for insurance loss, risk and exposure?

##Hypothesis & Predictions
##Hypothesis: Tornadoes are shifting geographically with increasing frequency in the Dixie Alley region of the United States and decreasing in frequency in the Tornado Alley region. This shift should increase the risk of insurance exposure and loss due to the higher population density and greater concentration of housing and properties in the Southeast. 

##Prediction: The county-level analysis of historical tornado data is expected to show the suggested research that there is a shift of increasing tornado frequency in counties within the Southeast. Trends in the Great Plains should also indicate whether this shows a geographic shift and decline in frequency in this region or an expansion of tornado risk with tornado frequency remaining either stable or increasing in activity within both regions. When combined with the analysis of the Census data that counties in the Southeast's Dixie Alley region have a higher population density thus resulting in a higher exposure and risk for insurance loss in comparison with the Tornado Alley region.
